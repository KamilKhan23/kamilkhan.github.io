About Me

I am a computer systems engineering student and game developer with a research interest in computational media, procedural generation, and expressive interactive systems. I work at the intersection of technical systems and player experience, exploring how artificial agents, environments, and rules can communicate meaning through behavior rather than explicit narrative. My background spans game development, augmented and virtual reality, and AI driven systems, with projects ranging from procedural world generation to non verbal interaction experiments. Having grown up across the United States, Canada, and Pakistan, I bring a perspective shaped by movement, adaptation, and access, and I am particularly interested in how interactive systems foster curiosity, interpretation, and connection. My long term goal is to pursue research in computational media, designing systems that use computation to expand how people play, think, and experience interactive worlds.

BS Computer Systems Engineering @ UET Peshawar Expected 2026

Research Interests

My research interests center on Computational Media, Game AI, and Expressive Interaction Systems, with emphasis on:

  • Procedural Content Generation (PCG) & adaptive world systems
  • Agent behavior, autonomy, and player system relationships
  • Non verbal, interpretive, and affect driven interaction
  • AR perception & real time inference for embodied play
  • Meaning formation through system response rather than explicit narrative

I aim to explore how digital systems can communicate emotion, familiarity, and trust through behavior, memory, and environment and how interactive media can evoke understanding without words.
